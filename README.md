# Healthcare-analysis Report

# Executive Summary
This analysis was conducted to examine patient trends, hospital revenue performance, admission patterns, medical conditions, and insurance contributions within the healthcare dataset using Pivot Tables and Pivot Charts.
The findings indicate that senior citizens form the largest patient group, chronic diseases account for a significant portion of medical cases, and insurance partnerships contribute heavily to hospital revenue. The analysis also suggests relatively stable monthly revenue performance, reflecting consistent healthcare demand and operational stability.
# Key Findings, Observations, Insights & Recommendations
# 1. Gender Distribution of Patients
# Observation:
The dataset recorded 27,496 male patients and 27,470 female patients, showing an almost equal gender distribution.
 Insight:
This indicates balanced healthcare utilization across both genders, suggesting that healthcare demand is not significantly gender-specific.
 Recommendation:
The hospital should continue maintaining balanced healthcare programs and equitable resource allocation across patient groups.
# 2. Senior Citizens Represent the Largest Patient Group
 Observation:
Senior citizens recorded the highest number of admissions with 32,445 patients.
Insight:
This suggests that age-related illnesses and chronic health conditions are major drivers of healthcare demand within the hospital.
 Recommendation:
The hospital should strengthen geriatric healthcare services and expand long-term disease management programs.
# 3. Elective Admissions Generated the Highest Revenue
Observation:
Elective admissions generated the highest revenue at approximately ₦7.463 trillion.
Insight:
Planned procedures and scheduled treatments contribute significantly to the hospital’s financial performance due to better resource planning and service organization.
Recommendation:
Management should invest more in specialized elective procedures and improve appointment scheduling systems.
# 4. Emergency Admissions Generated the Lowest Revenue
Observation:
Emergency admissions generated the lowest revenue compared to elective and urgent admissions.
Insight:
Emergency healthcare services focus mainly on urgent patient stabilization and immediate treatment needs rather than planned procedures.
Recommendation:
The hospital should improve emergency response efficiency and encourage preventive healthcare practices to reduce avoidable emergency visits.
# 5. Cigna was the Highest Revenue-Contributing Insurance Provider
Observation:
Cigna contributed approximately ₦4.529 trillion in billing revenue.
Insight:
This highlights the importance of insurance partnerships in sustaining hospital revenue and healthcare accessibility.
Recommendation:
The hospital should strengthen partnerships with high-performing insurance providers and negotiate improved reimbursement agreements.
# 6. Chronic Diseases Dominated Medical Cases
Observation:
Arthritis, Diabetes, Hypertension, Obesity, and Cancer were among the most common medical conditions recorded in the dataset.
Insight:
The high occurrence of chronic illnesses suggests increasing healthcare demand linked to aging and lifestyle-related health conditions.
Recommendation:
The hospital should expand preventive healthcare campaigns, regular screenings, and chronic disease management programs.
# 7. Arthritis Patients Recorded the Longest Hospital Stay
Observation:
Patients diagnosed with Arthritis recorded the longest average hospital stay duration.
Insight:
Longer hospital stays may indicate increased demand for rehabilitation, pain management, and continuous medical support.
Recommendation:
The hospital should improve physiotherapy, rehabilitation services, and outpatient recovery programs.
# 8. Monthly Revenue Trend Indicates Relative Financial Stability
Observation:
Monthly hospital revenue remained relatively stable throughout the observed period.
Insight:
This suggests steady patient demand and consistent operational performance across different months.
Recommendation:
Management should continue maintaining service quality and use revenue trends for future planning and forecasting.
# 9. Blood Group Distribution was Fairly Balanced
Observation:
Blood group distribution among patients remained relatively balanced across all blood types.
Insight:
This indicates a diverse patient population without strong dominance from a single blood group category.
Recommendation:
The hospital’s blood bank should maintain balanced blood inventory levels and strengthen blood donation awareness programs.
# 10. Healthcare Demand is Strongly Influenced by Aging and Chronic Illnesses
Observation:
Senior citizens and chronic disease cases accounted for a large proportion of hospital admissions.
Insight:
Healthcare demand is gradually shifting toward long-term disease management and elderly healthcare services.
Recommendation:
The hospital should invest more in preventive healthcare, elderly care infrastructure, and long-term treatment support systems.
# Overall Conclusion
The analysis revealed that chronic illnesses and elderly patients contribute significantly to hospital admissions and healthcare demand. Elective admissions generated the highest revenue, while insurance providers such as Cigna contributed heavily to the hospital’s financial performance.
Overall, the findings demonstrate how data-driven insights can support better healthcare planning, efficient resource allocation, and improved operational decision-making within the healthcare sector.
